# Consumption

 - defined as the use of goods and services by a household
- measured by [[GDP]]

## Types

1. Durable vs non-durable (based on durability)
2. Essential vs Discretionary (basic survival or luxurious)
3. Public vs Private
4. Sustainable vs Unsustainable (able to meet the present goals without putting the future goals at risk)

## Importance

1. The beginning of all economic activity - 
2. End of economic activities - if a person decides to eat a sandwich, all of the work and effort to make that sandwich was directed to the consumer’s consumption
3. Consumption drives production - 
4. Economic theories - how behavior affects inputs in the economy (economic growth and stability)
5. Government theories - designing effective policies


## Factors influing the consumption

1. Income
2. Prices
3. Interest Rates

Other than these, there are psychological, social and cultural factors


## Classical perspectives on consumption

1. Mostly focused on supply side factors
2. Classicals didn’t focus much on consumption
3. Say’s law: “Supply creates its own demand”
4. David Ricardo: suggested that saving was better than consumiing
5. John Stuart Milton: suggested that not consuming will cause economic slow-down


## Keynes view on consumption

1. Introduced the concept of consumption function - relationship between consumption and income


## Neoclassical economist’s view

1. consumption is a central concept
2. per-person value is an important indicator for growth
