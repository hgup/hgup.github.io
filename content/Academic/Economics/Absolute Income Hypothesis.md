What is the factor that actually drives the consumption in the economy?


# Intro

- Closed economic identity: $Y = C+ I + G$
- $C$ constitutes the largest portion of $Y$
- Accounts for $50\%$ to $70\%$  of GDP
- Prior to Keynes
	- Rate of interest and consumption has a negative relationship
- Keynes
	- If there is a change in rate of interest, it doesn’t mean we will change our way of living.
	- Fundamental Psychological Law [^1]
		- Consumption = F(Disposable Income)
# Absolute income Hypothesis

$$
C_t = a+ bY_t
$$
- $a > 0$ and $0 < b < 1$, the marginal propensity to consume
- based on Fundamental Psychological Law

## Proposition of the law

1. $Y$ increases $\to$ $C$ increases but by a somewhat smaller amount:
	- MPC < 1
